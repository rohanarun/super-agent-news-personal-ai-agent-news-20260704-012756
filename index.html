This week in personal AI agents

Gemini pushes onto the desktop

Google rolled out Gemini Spark on macOS, positioning it as a desktop agent that can work across local files, connected apps, and longer-running tasks. This marks a clear escalation from chat toward persistent computer interaction.

Source: blog.google

ChatGPT leans into agent futures

OpenAI leadership continues to frame ChatGPT as converging with agents, predicting a future where interfaces fade and intent-driven agents take over. The direction is clear, even if durable computer-use workflows remain early.

Source: 36Kr

Siri, Grok, and consumer pressure

Coverage this week highlights renewed attention on Siri’s AI progress and Grok’s expansion onto consumer surfaces like CarPlay — reinforcing how personal agents are becoming a mainstream expectation.

Source: bgr.com

Enterprises standardise agents

From Cisco to SAP-adjacent ecosystems, large organisations are deploying internal AI assistants broadly — validating demand, while also exposing the limits of one-off, non-reusable automation.

Source: cio.com

The personal AI agent landscape

ChatGPT

A world-class conversational assistant evolving toward agents, orchestration, and scheduled tasks. Exceptional for reasoning, writing, and ad‑hoc help.

Gemini

Google’s aggressive push into computer use via Gemini Spark shows how central desktop and browser control has become.

Siri

Voice-first and deeply embedded in Apple devices, with growing expectations around on-device intelligence and privacy.

Grok

An opinionated assistant tied to real-time and social context, now expanding across consumer platforms.

Folk

Primarily known for CRM-centric workflows, Folk sits adjacent to the agent conversation as teams explore automation inside relationship tools.

Orchids

Experimental approaches to automation and agents signal ongoing exploration, though with limited public updates this week.
